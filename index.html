    How scoring works (and what it can't tell you)

    RezGap extracts the most important keywords and phrases from the job posting (weighted by how often and how prominently they appear), then checks which ones your resume actually contains. The score is the weighted share your resume covers. It measures keyword overlap, the same thing recruiters' ATS searches check for. It doesn't judge your experience, and it can't guarantee an interview. And despite the scare stat you may have seen elsewhere, most ATS don't auto-reject resumes. They store them, and recruiters search and rank them by keywords. That search is what this score approximates. It also doesn't understand synonyms: if the posting says "Google Analytics" and you wrote "web analytics tooling," add their exact words where they're true of you. Never add keywords that aren't true.
